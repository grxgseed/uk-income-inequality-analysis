# UK Household Income & Inequality Analysis (1977–2024)

## Overview
This project explores long-term trends in UK household income and inequality using data from the Office for National Statistics (ONS).

An Excel dashboard was developed to analyse changes in disposable income over time, compare different household groups, and examine income distribution across the population.

---

## Objectives
- Analyse trends in UK household disposable income from 1977 to 2024  
- Compare income levels between working and retired households  
- Examine income distribution using quartiles and decile thresholds  
- Assess changes in income inequality over time  

---

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel  
  - Data cleaning and structuring  
  - Calculated metrics (e.g. inequality gap, year-on-year change)  
  - Data visualisation (line charts, dashboard design)  

---

## Key Analysis

### 1. Income Trends by Household Type
- Compared mean disposable income for working and retired households over time  
- Analysed year-on-year percentage changes in income  

### 2. Income Distribution (Quartiles)
- Compared top and bottom income quartiles  
- Calculated the income gap between highest and lowest groups  

### 3. Income Distribution (Deciles)
- Analysed income thresholds across the distribution  
- Focused on lower (2nd), median (5th), and upper (10th) deciles  

---

## Key Insights
- Income inequality has remained a persistent feature of the UK economy over time  
- Higher-income households have generally experienced stronger long-term income growth  
- The gap between top and bottom income groups remains substantial  
- The rate of change in inequality fluctuates, indicating periods of both widening and narrowing gaps
